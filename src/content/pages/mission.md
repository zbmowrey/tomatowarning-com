# Our Mission

*To help communities across the country prepare and recover from weather events by using our profits to buy and deliver food, water, and other necessities to recovery organizations in affected areas.*

## How It Started

It started during a summer storm in Southern Illinois, when a three-year-old boy named Max looked up at his dad and said, *"Daddy, there's a Tomato Warning."* 

He meant tornado. But in that moment, something clicked. Tomato Warning became more than a mispronunciation — it became a name, a brand, and eventually, an uncompromising lineup of premium hot salsas.

We moved to Florida shortly after that storm, but the weather has a way of following you. Between the hurricanes on the coast, the devastation we've witnessed in eastern Tennessee and western North Carolina, and the seasonal destruction across Tornado Alley, our mission couldn't remain just about flavor. We are here to help communities prepare, endure, and recover.

## Supporting Our Communities

### In the Worst of Times

When a community is hit by severe weather, the fastest, most effective help usually comes from the people already living there. We believe in supporting regional and local disaster response nonprofits — the teams whose portfolios are intimately tied to storm response and recovery operations. 

We provide direct monetary and supply donations to the teams putting boots on the ground when critical weather events occur. We don't do vague "portion of proceeds" marketing. Instead, **we target at least 10% of our net profits** to directly help communities recover from extreme weather. 

### ...And the Best of Times

Community isn't just about surviving the storms; it's about thriving in the sunshine. We work with local schools and clubs to support their fundraising efforts by providing Tomato Warning salsas at or below our cost. 

This ensures local groups have access to a premium, highly marketable product to raise money for children growing and learning about the world around them — all without detracting from our primary mission of funding top-line disaster relief.

# Tomato Warning: Marketing Strategy & Campaign Framework

## Marketing Philosophy

**We build brand and drive revenue simultaneously. Never one without the other.**

Every marketing dollar must do double duty: build long-term brand equity AND generate measurable, near-term commercial results. We reject the false choice between "brand marketing" and "performance marketing." A social post that builds brand love but doesn't drive action is half a job. A paid ad that converts but erodes brand equity is a loan against our future.

**Our marketing principles:**

1. **Lead with flavor, follow with mission.** For the primary buyer, taste is the qualifier; the charitable mission is the loyalty driver. Never lead with the mission in isolation -- it must be earned through product quality.
2. **The product is playful; the mission is earnest. Never mix them up.** Storm metaphors, EF-scale banter, and heat challenges are fun. Disaster relief is serious. Marketing must maintain this tonal firewall.
3. **Consistency across every touchpoint.** A customer who discovers us on TikTok, visits the website, buys at a farmers market, and receives a shipping confirmation email should feel like they're talking to the same brand every time.
4. **Measure everything, but don't worship the dashboard.** Data informs creative decisions; it doesn't make them. We track KPIs rigorously, but we also trust our instincts when the data is incomplete -- which it will be in Year 1.
5. **Earn attention before we buy it.** Organic reach first. Build a genuine community. When we invest in paid channels, we amplify content that's already proven organically.

## Social Media Playbook

### Platform Strategy

#### Instagram
- **Role:** Primary brand home. Visual storytelling, food photography, lifestyle content.
- **Voice:** Confident, flavorful, visually stunning. Think food magazine meets weather channel. Polished but not sterile.
- **Content mix:** 40% food/recipe content, 25% product + EF-scale education, 20% behind-the-scenes / founder story, 15% mission + community impact.
- **Format priorities:** Reels (algorithm-favored, discoverable), carousel posts (educational, saveable), Stories (daily engagement, polls, BTS).
- **Posting cadence:** 4-5 feed posts/week, daily Stories, 2-3 Reels/week.
- **Engagement rules:** Respond to every comment within 4 hours during business hours. Never ignore negative feedback -- address it directly and with warmth. Repost UGC enthusiastically and tag creators.
- **Call-to-action strategy:** Bio link to website. Every third post drives a specific action (shop, sign up, share).

#### TikTok
- **Role:** Discovery engine. Virality, heat challenges, behind-the-scenes, personality.
- **Voice:** Looser, more human, slightly more irreverent. Still on-brand but less polished. Show the humans behind the jars.
- **Content mix:** 35% heat challenges / taste tests, 25% recipe hacks, 20% founder story / BTS, 10% trends (only when they fit naturally), 10% mission / impact.
- **Format priorities:** Short-form video (15-60 seconds), trending audio when relevant, duets/stitches with food creators.
- **Posting cadence:** 5-7 videos/week. Volume matters on TikTok.
- **Key series concepts:**
  - "EF Challenge" -- taste your way from EF-1 to EF-5, rate the heat
  - "Storm Chasers" -- people trying Ground Zero (EF-5) for the first time
  - "Jar to Table" -- fast recipe videos using each EF level
  - "How It Started" -- origin story clips (Max saying "Tomato Warning")
- **Engagement rules:** Reply to comments with video when possible. Engage with hot sauce / food communities genuinely. Never buy followers or engagement.

#### Facebook
- **Role:** Community hub, mission storytelling, local marketing, 35+ demographic.
- **Voice:** Warmer, more conversational, slightly more mission-forward than other platforms.
- **Content mix:** 30% mission / community impact, 25% local events + farmers market updates, 25% recipe content, 20% product + promotions.
- **Format priorities:** Link posts to blog/website, event promotions, community group engagement, longer-form storytelling posts.
- **Posting cadence:** 3-4 posts/week. Quality over quantity.
- **Community building:** Create a "Storm Chasers" Facebook Group for superfans. Use it for early product feedback, recipe sharing, and building a tribe.
- **Local focus:** Event announcements, farmers market schedules, local partnership spotlights.

### Social Media Do's and Don'ts

*All social content must pass the seven-point Voice Test from the Brand Voice & Tone Guide (docs/business/brand-voice-and-tone.md). When in doubt, return to The Golden Rule: the product is playful; the mission is earnest.*

**Do:**
- Use storm metaphors playfully when talking about products
- Celebrate customer content (repost, engage, amplify)
- Be specific about heat levels -- the EF system is a key differentiator
- Show real people, real food, real moments
- Acknowledge storms and disasters with sincerity and mission focus
- Use sentence fragments and short punchy copy (per voice guide)
- Limit hashtags to 1-2 per post, used intentionally
- Limit emojis to one per post maximum, if any

**Don't:**
- Make light of actual weather disasters in product posts
- Use the mission as a sales tactic ("buy our salsa or disaster victims suffer")
- Over-use the origin story -- it's powerful because it's rare
- Chase trends that don't fit the brand just for reach
- Post generic "happy [holiday]" content without brand relevance
- Respond defensively to criticism -- own mistakes, fix them publicly
- Use exclamation points in headlines or subject lines
- Use any banned terms from the vocabulary bible ("artisanal," "curated," "elevated," "journey," "game-changer," "world's best")

## Email Marketing Blueprint

### Segmentation Strategy

**By persona:**
- Flavor-forward foodies: More product content, recipes, new flavor announcements
- Health-conscious readers: Ingredient spotlights, clean label content, sourcing stories
- Gift/occasion buyers: Gift guides, seasonal bundles, occasion reminders

**By behavior:**
- Engaged subscribers (open 50%+ of emails): Early access, VIP offers, feedback requests

- Purchasers: Post-purchase sequences, cross-sell, loyalty content

**By heat preference:**
- EF-1/EF-2 buyers: Accessible content, recipe focus, gentle upsell to next level

## PR & Media Strategy

### Press Narrative Angles

**Primary angles (ranked by newsworthiness):**

1. **"Kid says 'Tomato Warning,' dad builds a salsa empire."** The origin story. Irresistible human interest. Every food editor and morning show producer loves a founder story with a hook this good.

2. **"Extreme heat for extreme good: the salsa that funds disaster relief."** The mission angle. 500,000 SHU + 10% of net profits to disaster relief. This is the EF-5 Ground Zero PR hero story. **Naming sensitivity note:** "Ground Zero" carries a 9/11 association risk (flagged in Content Strategy, Section 9.4). PR team must have a prepared, empathetic response ready for when the association is raised publicly. If the Brand Council decides to rename, the PR strategy adapts accordingly.

3. **"The EF-scale for salsa: finally, a heat rating system that works."** The innovation angle. Solving the "is this salsa actually hot?" problem. Appeals to food media and consumer tech/innovation outlets.

4. **"Gulf Coast founder turns storm culture into a brand."** The regional angle. Local media in FL, TX, OK. Tornado Alley cultural relevance.

5. **"Not your average fundraiser: premium salsa replacing cookie dough."** The fundraising channel angle. Education, nonprofit, and community media.

## Influencer & Creator Partnerships

### Partnership Philosophy

We partner with creators who genuinely love food, heat, or community impact. We never ask anyone to pretend they like something they don't. Authenticity is non-negotiable. We'd rather have 10 creators who genuinely love our product than 100 who post once for a paycheck.

### Creator Tiers

| Tier | Follower Range | Compensation | Expectation | Use Case |
|------|---------------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Micro | 1K-10K | Free product + affiliate code | 1-2 organic posts | Grassroots, local, authentic |
| Mid | 10K-100K | Free product + flat fee ($200-500) + affiliate | 2-3 posts + Stories | Regional reach, food niche |
| Macro | 100K-1M | Negotiated fee + product + affiliate | Campaign-level partnership | Major launches, awareness |
| Celebrity/Media | 1M+ | Custom negotiation | One-time or ongoing | Halo effect, PR amplification |

### Selection Criteria

**Must-have:**
- Genuine interest in food, cooking, or hot sauce culture
- Audience overlap with our target segments (age, interests, geography)
- Content quality that matches our brand aesthetic (or complements it)
- Engagement rate > 2% (follower count is vanity; engagement is sanity)

**Nice-to-have:**
- Gulf Coast / Tornado Alley geography
- Previous mission/cause advocacy
- Existing hot sauce or spicy food content
- Diverse audience demographics

**Disqualifiers:**
- History of controversial behavior that conflicts with our mission values
- Audience composed primarily of bots or purchased followers
- Unwillingness to disclose the partnership (FTC compliance is non-negotiable)
- Content style that's significantly off-brand (overly crude, mean-spirited)

### Creative Guidelines for Creators

- Show the product in real cooking/eating situations -- not staged unboxing only
- Mention the EF-scale and heat level -- it's a key differentiator
- Encouraged (not required) to mention the charitable mission
- Use branded hashtags: #TomatoWarning #RatedByTheStorm
- Disclose the partnership per FTC guidelines (always, every time, no exceptions)
- We provide a brief, not a script. Authenticity requires creative freedom.

### Farmers Markets

**Why farmers markets matter in Year 1:**
Farmers markets are not just a sales channel -- they are a research lab, a content studio, and a brand-building machine. Direct consumer feedback, email list building, social content capture, and real-time product testing happen simultaneously.

**Booth strategy:**
- **Visual design:** Storm charcoal backdrop, EF-scale color gradient display, branded tablecloth, professional signage. Must look premium -- not like a hobby brand.
- **Sampling:** Offer all five EF levels. Use the tasting as a narrative journey: start at EF-1, let them work up. This IS the brand experience.
- **Email capture:** Tablet or printed QR code for email signup. Incentive: free recipe card or 10% off first online order.
- **Social content:** Photograph/video reactions (with permission). Farmers market content is some of the most authentic, shareable content we can create.
- **Merchandise display:** Full product lineup + gift sets + branded merchandise (stickers at minimum).
- **Signage:** Clear pricing, EF-scale explainer, mission statement. QR code to website.

**Target markets (Gulf Coast FL launch):**
- Sarasota Farmers Market (Saturday)
- St. Pete Saturday Morning Market
- Tampa Heights Night Market
- Fort Myers Farmers Market
- Naples Third Street South Farmers Market

**Success metrics per event:**
- Jars sold
- Email signups collected
- Social content captured (photos, video, testimonials)
- Customer feedback notes (qualitative)
- Revenue per event

## Metrics & Measurement

### North Star Metric (Year 1)

**Email list size and quality (engaged subscribers).**

Rationale: In Year 1, our list IS our future revenue. Every subscriber represents a direct relationship we own (not rented from a social platform). Email list growth is the leading indicator of sustainable revenue.

Secondary north star: Revenue per customer (measures product-market fit and repeat purchase behavior).

### Attribution Approach

**Year 1 model: Simplified attribution**
- **First-touch** for awareness channels (social, PR, influencer): How did they find us?
- **Last-touch** for conversion channels (email, DTC, events): What closed the sale?
- **UTM discipline:** Every link, every campaign, every channel gets a unique UTM. No exceptions.
- **Post-purchase survey:** "How did you hear about us?" on order confirmation. Simple, directionally accurate.
- **Promo codes by channel:** Unique codes for each influencer, event, and campaign for clean tracking.

**Year 2+: Multi-touch**
- Implement multi-touch attribution as volume and data mature
- Consider tools like Triple Whale or Northbeam for DTC attribution

## Cross-Document Dependencies

This marketing strategy must align with and is governed by the following brand documents:

| Document | Relationship | Key Alignment Points |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| Brand Voice & Tone Guide | Voice and tone rules govern all marketing copy | Golden Rule, vocabulary bible, banned terms, seven-point Voice Test |
| Messaging Framework & Copy Guidelines | Persona messaging, product claims, and CTAs are sourced from here | Persona messages, product messaging matrix, CTA library, anti-messages |
| Storytelling & Content Strategy | Content guardrails and crisis protocols apply to all marketing content | Active weather emergency protocol, pre-profit charitable claim rules, UGC guardrails, screenshot test |
| Brand Story Bible | Origin story in four lengths (15s, 60s, 3min, full), product narratives, brand archetypes (Creator/Outlaw/Caregiver), story adaptation guidelines by channel | Campaign creative should map to archetypes: Creator = flavor content, Outlaw = heat challenge content, Caregiver = mission content |
| Sales Strategy & Playbook | Marketing-sales handoffs must match; variety pack names and pricing are sourced from here | Fundraiser channel materials, retail pitch support, lead qualification, DTC pricing and bundles |
| Customer Communication & Engagement Playbook | Post-purchase and customer touchpoint messaging; authoritative source for email sequence timing and content | Email sequences (7-email post-purchase flow), community management, crisis communication, review response strategy, FAQ library |

**Inconsistency between these documents is a brand risk.** All marketing campaigns, email sequences, social content, and PR materials must be cross-referenced against the governing documents listed above. When documents conflict, escalate to the Brand Council for resolution.

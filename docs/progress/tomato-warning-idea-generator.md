## Category 1: Product Line

### Core EF-Scale Lineup (EF-1 through EF-5)

---

**EF-1: Coastal Calm**
- Flavor profile: Mild, bright, fresh-forward. Ripe Florida tomatoes, sweet onion, poblano pepper, cilantro, lime, sea salt. No bell pepper filler — fresh herb brightness instead.
- Scoville range commitment: 500–1,500 SHU
- Ingredients direction: Roma and beefsteak tomatoes, poblano, sweet white onion, fresh cilantro, lime juice, sea salt, garlic
- Heat descriptor on label: "A gentle gust — big flavor, little fire"
- User need addressed: Consumers frustrated by heat inconsistency across brands; EF-scale with a committed Scoville range solves this directly. Also captures the health-conscious ingredient reader who wants real flavor without punishment.
- Evidence: "No FDA/USDA standard for mild/medium/hot — consumers frustrated by inconsistency across brands. EF-scale with Scoville-range commitment solves a real problem." (Research, Market Gaps #3)
- Estimated effort: Small (baseline variety, establishes formula)
- Estimated impact: High (gateway SKU; entry point for casual buyers and the health-conscious segment)

---

**EF-2: Gulf Breeze**
- Flavor profile: Medium-mild. Roasted tomato base with chipotle for smoke depth, serrano for the first real heat spike, roasted garlic, and a hint of apple cider vinegar for brightness.
- Scoville range commitment: 1,500–5,000 SHU
- Ingredients direction: Fire-roasted tomatoes, chipotle in adobo, serrano peppers, roasted garlic, sweet onion, ACV, salt
- Heat descriptor on label: "Building on the horizon — smoke, depth, and your first real tingle"
- User need addressed: Flavor-forward foodie wants authentic flavor that mass brands have dumbed down. The chipotle smoke separates this from generic "medium" salsas immediately.
- Evidence: "Flavor dumbing-down (HIGH severity) — Mass brands optimized for inoffensiveness at the cost of authentic flavor." (Research, Pain Points #2)
- Estimated effort: Small
- Estimated impact: High (likely the best-selling SKU — sits in the sweet spot of most buyers' preference)

---

**EF-3: Squall Line**
- Flavor profile: Medium-hot. Habanero-forward heat with a tropical counter (mango or pineapple) to balance the punch. Distinctly complex — the heat arrives after the sweetness. Classic "gateway heat" for heat explorers.
- Scoville range commitment: 5,000–25,000 SHU
- Ingredients direction: Habanero peppers, fire-roasted tomatoes, fresh mango or pineapple, red onion, lime, cilantro, garlic
- Heat descriptor on label: "The storm is here — tropical sweetness chased by real heat"
- User need addressed: Heat exploration is at peak consumer interest; this SKU converts flavor-forward foodies into heat challengers. The persona "Marcus, 34" explicitly "buys EF-3 first, challenges himself with EF-5."
- Evidence: "Heat exploration at peak consumer interest — Ghost Pepper, Carolina Reaper now mainstream premium SKUs." (Research, Tailwinds #1); Marcus persona directly references EF-3 as first purchase.
- Estimated effort: Small-Medium (fruit ingredient sourcing adds complexity)
- Estimated impact: High (social media-shareable flavor combination; strong gift set anchor)

---

**EF-4: Supercell**
- Flavor profile: Hot. Ghost pepper and red fresno blend — the ghost pepper delivers sustained, deep heat; fresno keeps the color vivid red and adds a clean, sharp heat note. Black garlic adds umami depth.
- Scoville range commitment: 25,000–100,000 SHU
- Ingredients direction: Ghost peppers, red fresno chiles, fire-roasted tomatoes, black garlic, white onion, cumin, salt
- Heat descriptor on label: "Rotation confirmed — deep, relentless, rewarding"
- User need addressed: Mrs. Renfro's Ghost Pepper was their fastest-growing product ever, proving heat-as-identity drives both purchase and media attention. This SKU is the prestige tier for heat adventurers.
- Evidence: "Mrs. Renfro's Ghost Pepper Salsa was the fastest-growing new product in company history — validating that heat-as-novelty/identity is a proven commercial strategy." (Research, Competitive Analysis)
- Estimated effort: Medium (ghost pepper sourcing, safety in production)
- Estimated impact: High (PR magnet; drives trial of lower EF levels; social content fuel)

---

**EF-5: Ground Zero**
- Flavor profile: Extreme. Carolina Reaper and Trinidad Moruga Scorpion blend. Not novelty heat for shock value — designed to have actual salsa character (charred tomatoes, roasted onion, depth) with catastrophic heat. A real salsa that happens to be extreme.
- Scoville range commitment: 100,000–500,000+ SHU (clearly labeled)
- Ingredients direction: Carolina Reaper, Trinidad Moruga Scorpion, charred Roma tomatoes, roasted yellow onion, smoked paprika, garlic, lime
- Heat descriptor on label: "Total devastation — for the few who dare. Not responsible for consequences."
- Safety note on label: Serve in small amounts. Dairy nearby recommended.
- User need addressed: The heat explorer segment (Marcus persona) explicitly "challenges himself with EF-5." This SKU is the PR/media hero and the aspirational cap of the EF system — it makes EF-1 and EF-2 purchases feel like the beginning of a journey.
- Evidence: Research recommendation #10: "Consider launching EF-5 as the PR/media hero — extreme heat + extreme charity = shareable story." Also: heat exploration trend (Tailwinds #1).
- Estimated effort: Medium-Large (extreme pepper sourcing, liability labeling, media strategy around launch)
- Estimated impact: High (drives earned media, defines brand ceiling, generates social content organically)

### Specialty / Seasonal Storm-Themed Varieties

---

**Specialty 1: Hurricane Season (Tropical Mango-Habanero Verde)**
- Description: A Florida-specific limited edition launching each June 1 (official start of hurricane season). Green tomatillo base with fresh mango, habanero, and key lime. Bright, tropical, and distinctly Floridian. Label features a swirling tropical storm radar image. A portion of proceeds from Hurricane Season goes specifically to Gulf Coast hurricane relief (partner: Direct Relief).
- User need addressed: Regional identity as an asset — Gulf Coast FL consumers have a visceral relationship with hurricane culture. This SKU is hyperlocal and seasonal, creating urgency and collector appeal.
- Evidence: "Regional food identity as asset — Publix Local Products program, H-E-B local brand support... demonstrate retailer appetite for regional brands." (Research, Tailwinds #5); Direct Relief named as possible partner in Research, Charitable Mission section.
- Estimated effort: Medium (seasonal production run, separate label, PR coordination)
- Estimated impact: High (annual earned media event, Florida retailer pitch hook, reinforces charitable mission)

---

**Specialty 2: Hailstorm (White Salsa / Roasted Garlic & Green Chile)**
- Description: A departure from red — a creamy-looking white/green salsa made with tomatillos, roasted Hatch green chiles, white onion, roasted garlic, and Mexican crema. The "hailstorm" name captures the unexpected color and the sharp, fast heat of green chile. Not cream-based — uses a tomatillo and crema emulsion.
- User need addressed: Visual differentiation from the red-salsa shelf. Flavor-forward foodies want to be surprised. White/green stands out immediately in a gift set.
- Evidence: "Boring branding (MEDIUM severity) — Category visually crowded with generic southwestern imagery." (Research, Pain Points #4); gift set differentiation opportunity.
- Estimated effort: Medium (distinct formulation, production run planning)
- Estimated impact: Medium-High (strong gift set inclusion, media hook as "unexpected" variety)

---

**Specialty 3: Black Squall (Blackened Tomato & Smoked Black Garlic)**
- Description: A dark, brooding salsa using blackened/charred tomatoes, smoked black garlic, dried ancho and mulato chiles, and dark chocolate mole notes. Medium heat (EF-2 range) but extreme depth. The visual is striking — nearly black-red with a dark label. Positioned as the "sophisticated" salsa for pairing with grilled meats.
- User need addressed: Flavor-forward foodies want authentic, complex flavor beyond tomato-and-pepper. The mole direction speaks to ingredient storytelling and culinary credibility.
- Evidence: "Flavor dumbing-down (HIGH severity) — Mass brands optimized for inoffensiveness at the cost of authentic flavor." (Research, Pain Points #2); clean label trend supports named, interesting ingredients.
- Estimated effort: Medium
- Estimated impact: Medium (strong gift inclusion, chef/foodie media appeal, pairs with Flavor Chaser persona)

---

**Specialty 4: Ice Storm (Cucumber-Jalapeño Salsa Fresca)**
- Description: A bright, cold-weather-adjacent "salsa fresca" style with cucumber, jalapeño, tomatillo, fresh dill, shallot, and lemon. EF-1 heat range. Serves the health-conscious segment who wants something lighter and the flavor-forward foodie who wants something unexpected. Green-white visual identity.
- User need addressed: Health-conscious ingredient reader wants clean, recognizable ingredients. This SKU is the most "clean label" of the lineup, almost more of a relish or pico.
- Evidence: "58% of consumers want clear ingredient origin information; 30% of F&B launches feature clean label claims." (Research, Tailwinds #2); Segment 2 (Health-Conscious Ingredient Reader) profile.
- Estimated effort: Small-Medium
- Estimated impact: Medium (captures health-conscious segment, strong summer seasonal)

---

**Specialty 5: Derecho (Roasted Corn & Chipotle)**
- Description: A chunky, Midwest-inspired salsa featuring fire-roasted sweet corn, chipotle peppers, red bell pepper, black beans, and smoked tomatoes. Derecho storms sweep across the Midwest plains — this SKU signals the brand's expansion story toward Tornado Alley. Medium heat (EF-2 range).
- User need addressed: Prepares the brand for Tornado Alley expansion (TX/OK) where this ingredient combination has regional resonance. Also serves the gift/occasion buyer who wants something "hearty" and distinctive.
- Evidence: "Tornado Alley — Expansion Market... Highest per-capita salsa consumption region in US. Consumers have existing relationship with weather/storm culture — EF-scale is locally resonant." (Research, Regional Dynamics)
- Estimated effort: Medium
- Estimated impact: Medium (Midwest/Plains expansion signal; chunky texture differentiates at shelf)

---

**Specialty 6: Waterspout (Florida Keys Citrus Salsa)**
- Description: A Florida-exclusive variety featuring Key lime, blood orange, shaved fennel, habanero, and red onion. Fresh, bright, and completely unexpected in the salsa category. "Waterspouts over the Keys" label art. EF-2 heat. Targets the Florida tourist/gift market and the high-end restaurant channel.
- User need addressed: Gift/occasion buyer wants "something impressive and unexpected." This is the most distinctive-tasting SKU in the lineup and would anchor a luxury gift set.
- Evidence: Segment 3 (Gift/Occasion Buyer): "Wants something impressive and unexpected; 'interesting gift' mindset." (Research, Customer Segments); affluent retirees and winter tourists as FL consumer base.
- Estimated effort: Medium-Large (citrus sourcing seasonality, premium positioning)
- Estimated impact: Medium-High (strong gift anchor, PR hook as "most unusual salsa," Florida tourism channel)

## Category 3: Brand Voice & Messaging

### Tagline Candidates

1. **"Rated by the storm."** — Short, proprietary, instantly communicates the EF scale. Implies authority on heat. Works on jars, ads, t-shirts.

2. **"Brace yourself."** — Double meaning: brace for the heat, brace for a storm. Playful without being exhausting. Versatile across heat levels.

3. **"Heat you can trust. Flavor you won't forget."** — Functional and emotional in one line. Directly addresses the heat inconsistency pain point.

4. **"From the storm that named us."** — Leads with the origin story. More narrative, works on mission page and longer formats.

5. **"EF-scale heat. Real-world flavor."** — Most literal. Useful for retail signage and shelf talkers where clarity beats cleverness.

6. **"Chasing flavor since the forecast."** — Playful, slightly whimsical. Suits social media and merchandise.

7. **"Five levels. No apologies."** — Attitude-forward. Works for EF-4 and EF-5 specifically; too aggressive as brand-wide tagline.

8. **"Born in a storm. Built for your table."** — Origin + utility. Works for longer formats like the mission page hero.

9. **"The weather forecast you actually want to see."** — Humor-forward. Social media native; too cute for the jar.

10. **"Premium salsa with purpose. Just look at the forecast."** — The full story in one sentence. Works for elevator pitches and email subject lines.

---

### Tone Spectrum

**Product pages (playful, confident, specific):**
The voice here should feel like a knowledgeable friend who takes flavor seriously but doesn't take itself too seriously. Scoville ranges stated plainly. Flavor descriptions evocative but not overwrought. Storm metaphors used purposefully, not exhaustively. Example:

> "EF-3: Squall Line. Habanero heat arrives on a wave of fresh mango — you'll taste the sweetness first, then the storm rolls in. Scoville range: 5,000–25,000. Don't say we didn't warn you."

**Mission page (earnest, grounded, specific):**
No marketing voice here. Plain language, real numbers, named partners. The origin story told warmly. Giving structure described without hedging. Example:

> "Our son called it a 'tomato warning.' We called it a company name. Five percent of net profits goes to [Named Partner], funding disaster recovery in the communities where storms actually land. Here's what that looked like last year: [dollar amount], [specific impact]."

**Social media (conversational, reactive, heat-forward):**
Shorter, punchier, willing to be funny. Heat challenge culture welcomed. Shares community content. Responds to comments in the same voice. Storm puns used sparingly so they land harder. Example:

> "Someone rated EF-5 a three-star because it was 'too spicy.' We're choosing to take that as a compliment. [image of jar]"

---

### Key Messaging Pillars

1. **Heat you can trust** — The EF scale solves a real problem. We commit to a Scoville range and we keep it.
2. **Flavor first, always** — The heat is real but it's not a stunt. Every EF level has a distinct, considered flavor profile.
3. **A story worth telling** — The origin is genuine. A child's mispronunciation. A Gulf Coast family. A brand with a real beginning.
4. **Buy a jar, fund recovery** — Specific. Named. Reported. Not vague. Your purchase does a specific thing.
5. **Made for this region** — Gulf Coast FL is home. The stories, the ingredients, the cause — all connected to the place.

---

### The Earnest/Playful Balance

The governing principle: the product is playful; the mission is earnest. Never mix them up. A jar label can have storm puns and attitude. The mission page should read like it was written by someone who has seen hurricane damage firsthand. The two tones coexist because they occupy different spaces — playing it earnest on the product page would feel flat; playing it playful on the mission page would feel callous.

## Category 5: Fundraising Partner Program

---

**Program Name: "Storm Chasers Fund"**

**Program Structure:**
- Organizations sign up via the nonprofit partner form on the website
- Minimum campaign size: 10 jars to start (low barrier); typical campaign target: 50-100 jars
- Margin: Organizations keep 50% of retail price per jar sold
- At $8.99 retail: organization keeps $4.50/jar. 100 jars = $450. 200 jars = $900 (hits Coach Dave's target).
- At the 6-jar bundle price ($44.95 retail): organization keeps $22.50/bundle. 40 bundles = $900.
- No upfront cost to organization — product shipped on consignment with a 30-day return window for unsold jars (returns paid by Tomato Warning at launch phase)
- Campaign length: 3-4 weeks (shelf-stable; no urgency pressure)
- Organization receives: marketing kit (flyer template, social post copy, email template), custom label option, real-time sales tracker

**Custom Label Concept:**
- Organization logo placed on a secondary front panel label that adheres over the bottom third of the Tomato Warning label (or a full custom label for larger orders)
- Label includes: organization name, campaign name, "Powered by Tomato Warning," EF level indicator
- Minimum order for custom label: 48 jars (one case)
- Custom label cost: Absorbed by Tomato Warning at launch phase as a differentiator (label stock + printing is low unit cost)

**Campaign Flow:**
1. Organization submits interest form → receives welcome email with program details within 48 hours
2. Program coordinator call (15 min) to confirm campaign goals, timeline, custom label preference
3. Jars shipped with marketing kit 10 days before campaign start
4. Organization sells over 3-4 weeks (door-to-door, workplace, events)
5. End of campaign: organization submits payment for sold jars (50% of retail); returns unsold jars via prepaid label
